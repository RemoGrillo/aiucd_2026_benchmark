Motivation

From experiment to infrastructure — 2024–2026

CONVERGENCE 01

LLMs enter the everyday

Large & multimodal models have moved from experiments to systematic integration in GLAM and Digital-Humanities workflows: access, description, indexing, research assistance.

CONVERGENCE 02

Benchmarks are saturating

General-purpose suites hit near-ceiling performance, eroding their discriminative value and pushing the field toward “frontier” evaluations.

So the question is not “which model is better?” —
but “what are we measuring, and at what epistemic cost?”

The Core Argument

A benchmark is never a neutral ruler

  • Benchmarks are technical and epistemic tools: they don't just check fit — they define what counts as “understanding”.
  • They enable comparability, but can mislead where correctness is contextual, interpretive, and inseparable from sources.
  • Headline scores overstate practical capability — they under-specify provenance, evidence and ambiguity.
  • And they rarely reflect end-to-end pipeline behaviour in real institutions.
THE GAP

Quantitative score
   ≠  operational usefulness

A recurring distance between what leaderboards reward and what cultural institutions actually need.

Part 2 · Images — Visual Question Answering

Accuracy decays as reasoning deepens

  • Early VQA (Antol 2015; AQUA): synthetic Q–A pairs, exact-match scoring → 35–60% reflects pattern exploitation, not visual reasoning.
  • New generation (VQArt-Bench): symbolic interpretation & narrative inference, zero-shot.
  • Accuracy degrades systematically as reasoning complexity rises — models stay anchored to surface features.

Cross-Cutting Methodology · 1

Can we even trust the score?

VALIDITY THREAT

Contamination & memorization

Public CH sources are plausibly in pre-training data; some models reconstruct test sets from memory, not reasoning.

→ Anti-leakage strategies, e.g. never-web-indexed reference documents.

ROBUSTNESS AXIS

Primary-source noise

OCR errors & layout effects treated as an explicit robustness axis — not assumed away.

→ Keep the task fixed, vary the representation, localize where pipelines break.

The Bifurcation

The field is quietly splitting in two

  • Massive benchmarks for training & stress-testing (Museum-65) — optimize for base capability.
  • Small, high-validity benchmarks for humanities fidelity (CulturalVQA, HistBench) — optimize for practice fidelity.
  • The community is implicitly choosing one or the other.
  • Bridging them needs end-to-end pipelines, specialized rubrics, anti-contamination.

Gaps & Points of Intervention

Isolated scores ≠ whole-pipeline performance

1

Vary input conditions

Clean / noisy / image as standard practice, not an afterthought.

2

Evaluate structured tasks against needs

Against modelling assumptions & user requirements — not just whether a query executes.

3

Allow warranted alternatives

For interpretive tasks, accept plural answers rather than forcing a single gold.

4

Report operational constraints

Cost, latency, maintainability as first-class metrics.

Conclusion

The question isn't whether models are “good” in the abstract.

It is whether full workflows produce outputs that stay inspectable and defensible in humanities settings.

Without provenance, uncertainty and end-to-end behaviour, benchmarks reward test-shaped behaviour — not systems we can responsibly adopt.
